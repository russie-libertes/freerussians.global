On November 17 to 24, 2024 anti-war and democratic communities of Russians are holding a global rally.
November 19th marks 1000 days since Russian troops began their full-scale invasion of Ukraine. A thousand days of struggle, resilience and courage of Ukrainians who continue to fight for their freedom and independence. And a thousand days of anti-war Russians supporting Ukraine, helping the Ukrainian people to stand their ground and opposing Putin's regime, which waged a despicable war.
The action will begin on November 17 together with the march in Berlin, initiated by Russian politicians Ilya Yashin, Yulia Navalnaya and Vladimir Kara-Murza. The initiators invite everyone to participate in the Berlin event, whose main demands are: the immediate withdrawal of Russian troops from the territory of Ukraine and the establishment of a just peace with guarantees of non-aggression; the condemnation of Vladimir Putin as a war criminal; and the release of all political prisoners.
These demands will be supported at rallies of Russians in different parts of the world. At the protest events, Russians will honor the memory of war victims, demonstrate once again their solidarity with the people of Ukraine, recall the terrible consequences of a full-scale war, and the need for international support for Ukraine.
During the global campaign, funds will be raised around the world to help hospitals and schools in Ukraine as part of the Energy for Life project.

Energy for life

LET'S RAISING FUNDS FOR PORTABLE POWER PLANTS FOR UKRAINE

I will help Ukraine
Why it matters
Ongoing attacks on Ukraine's energy infrastructure mean millions suffer regular power outages. Hospitals are in a critical situation: without electricity, medical equipment cannot operate and patients' lives are at risk. Schools cannot provide a normal education for children. Temperatures can drop to -17C in winter.

How your help can make a difference
Your donations will help purchase and deliver portable generators to hospitals and schools in Ukraine, saving lives and allowing children to continue their studies even in war.
